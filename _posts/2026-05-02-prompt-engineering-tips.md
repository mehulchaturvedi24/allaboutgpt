Prompt engineering is the skill of crafting inputs that get the best possible output from GPT. Here are 10 practical tips to improve your results immediately.

---

## 1. Be Specific

Vague prompts produce vague answers. The more specific you are, the better GPT performs.

**Weak:** `Tell me about finance.`
**Strong:** `Explain the difference between IFRS 15 and IFRS 16 in simple terms with examples.`

---

## 2. Assign a Role

Tell GPT who to act as.

`You are a senior financial auditor. Review the following transaction and flag any risks.`

---

## 3. Set the Format

Ask for output in a specific format.

`Respond in a bullet-point list.`
`Give me the answer as a table with columns: Topic, Definition, Example.`

---

## 4. Provide Context

Give background information before asking your question.

`I am preparing a report for a construction company using SAP. The project is 60% complete. Help me write a progress summary.`

---

## 5. Use Step-by-Step Instructions

For complex tasks, ask GPT to think step by step.

`Think step by step and explain how revenue recognition works under IFRS 15 for a long-term contract.`

---

## 6. Set the Tone

`Write this in a professional tone for a board-level audience.`
`Explain this simply as if I am a 10-year-old.`

---

## 7. Limit the Length

`Summarize in 3 sentences.`
`Keep your answer under 200 words.`

---

## 8. Ask for Alternatives

`Give me 5 different ways to phrase this email subject line.`

---

## 9. Iterate and Refine

Don’t stop at the first answer. Ask follow-up questions or say:
`Make it more concise.`
`Now rewrite this in a more formal tone.`

---

## 10. Use Delimiters for Long Inputs

When pasting large blocks of text, wrap them clearly:

```
Summarize the following report:

---
[Paste your text here]
---
```

---

Mastering prompts takes practice, but these ten tips will give you a strong foundation immediately.
